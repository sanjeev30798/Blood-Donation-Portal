“Every day 12,000 people in India die due to the sheer lack of donated blood. India collects 11 million units of blood but needs 15 million units, so there's a deficit of 4 million units.”

"India has the world's largest shortage of blood, with all states together battling a huge shortfall of 41 million units and demand outstripping supply by over 400%, says the first of its kind study published in the journal The Lancet. And demand is rising, says the findings" read more

In the past few years, India has seen an improvement in the blood collection (from 9.8 million units in 2012-12 to 11.45 million in 2017-18).

Currently, India has 2,760 blood banks of which 1,131 blood banks are supported by NACO, Ministry of Health and Family Welfare (MoHFW).
